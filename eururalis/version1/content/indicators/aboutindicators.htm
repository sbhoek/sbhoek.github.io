EURURALIS' models simulated what the effects are of changes in boundary conditions for the various domains of sustainability : People, Planet, Profit. The selection of indicators depended on:

  • Issues of political interest. Recent EU documents were taken as entrance.
  • Data and causal relationships to assess changes for their effect should be available for the EU25 countries .
  • The need to avoid overdoses of information. Indicators have been chosen for their meaningfulness and their representativeness for other related aspects.


Some indicators, e.g. biodiversity were build upon partial indicators representing specific aspecsts (e.g. fragmentation effects, disturbance, pollution) or distinctive areas and specimens (agriculturally used ecosystems versus natural areas). Some were difficult to attribute exclusively to the 3P domain: animal diseases have a link to the Planet domain (living creatures), the Profit domain (losses in capital and income) and the People domain (acceptability of policy measures like culling). The resulting list is far from complete and some vital aspects could not be included. For example, data on various values of landscapes in Europe, for instance on cultural heritage, could not be included for reasons of data and methods lacking. Other data were only partly available (EU 15 countries instead of EU 25 in case of animal diseases.) In the Profit domain several additional data (off-farm income, contribution of recreation and tourism) would have been relevant, but sofar not available. Socio-cultural aspects would contribute to a more balanced input from the People domain, but where not at hand.


Built upon indicators we composed so-called Meta-indicators that give a more comprehensive or geeralised picture, see '7 Integration', tab 'meta-indicators'.
