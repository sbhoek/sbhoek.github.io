For EU 10 countries decreases of agricultural land in scenarios B1 and B2 are relatively modest due to EU support measures, the decrease in A1 is largest (10 %) For EU 15 countries the strongest decline is seen in the B scenarios


Built-up areas increase the most in scenario A1 ( 6.2 % of total land area in EU25, comparable with the complete area of the Netherlands), the least in B2 (4.3%) compared with a present land cover of 3.9%. A relatively large increase is observed in the EU 10 countries.


Bio fuels could occupy abandoned land. Huge areas are needed to meet EU goals for alternative energy use in the transport sector (4-13 % of agricultural area) Short term goals probably will not be met, energy efficiency so far is low.



Scenarios vary in relative importance of drivers, such as population growth and consumption patterns, support measures in the CAP that sustain or stimulate land use (especially in the B scenario's), developments in the world (or continental) market (especially related to the A1 and A2 scenarios). Agricultural land use furthermore reacts on the land demand from: interacting influences have been made explicit in scenarios and used in models. The model outcomes can be reviewed as follows:

  • In most scenarios a decrease in land demand from agriculture seems to reflect an ongoing trend of higher productivity per hectare as already seen in the past. Educated guesses point to the fact that these trends continue due to technological improvements and better management. This development runs parallel with demands for land for other goals.
  • In scenarios where EU support is important (B scenarios) and where the accession countries can take advantage of this a relatively low decrease in agricultural land use is to be expected. In the EU 15 countries this effect is much less. The B scenarios assume a more modest population growth, less welfare and lower consumption levels, liberalization scenarios assume the abolishment of support and demand for other products.
  • The A2 scenario remarkably diverges in agricultural land demand due to higher living standards related to higher incomes, whereas Europe tries to reach a higher degree of self-sufficiency. Cropland is used partly for animal fodder and increases. Farming intensities are relatively low.
  • Bio fuels are not yet included in land demand in the above figures. EU's targets to replace fossil fuels by bio fuels for 2010 for the transport sector assume a 5.75 % share. Translated to surfaces where bio fuels should be cultivated a substantial area, varying from 4 - 13 % should be realized. This figure, though not realistic on a short time horizon, could imply that the majority of agricultural land that otherwise could be abandoned could be used for bio fuels in the long term. The probability in economic terms and the low energy-efficientcy are both factors that obstruct such huge transformation.
  • Urban areas grow in all scenarios due to increase of population, higher living standards, demands from the industrial and services sector, personal mobility. For EU 25 the areas increase from 6.2 areal % in 2030 in A1 scenario to 4.3% in B2 scenario, compared with 3.9% at present. A relative strong increase can be observed in Central and Eastern Europe where urbanisation will be strongest (leading to a drain of rural communities. Strongest increase in EU10 countries occurs in A1 scenario (7.7 % of land areas).
  • Graphs show intriguing knicks around 2010. This can be attributed to the effects of CAP support measures until 2013 (see scenarios), incorporated in model results. EURUALIS has choosen 2010 as interval which explains why the change is then.
