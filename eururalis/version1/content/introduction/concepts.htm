EURURALIS conceptual anchors are:

  • policy orientation, interactive presentation , discussion tool
  • sustainability goals and evaluation criteria (People, Planet, Profit),
  • four contrasting, explorative scenarios,
  • land use change in the heart,
  • spatial and temporal specification,
  • core models fed with change in driving forces,
  • "what-if" answers expressed in 3P indicators,
  • policy evaluation

 

EURURALIS concepts


Policy discussion orientation:
Topics have been selected on current and foreseen policy relevance based upon analysis of recent EU-policy documents. EURURALIS is a discussion support, not a decision support. Facts and figures and transparency in what-if questions and answers are fundamental.

 

Specification in space and time
EURURALIS offers geographical detail through a nested approach, from global level, via EU-level, to national level, NUTS regions (when feasible and relevant) and at a high spatial resolution of 1 km2. Temporal specification is given for each 10 year interval. between 2000 and 2030 .

 

Scenario-approach
EURURALIS uses explorative, contrasting scenarios (four in number). We abstained from predictive scenarios. EURURALIS uses scientifically and politically accepted approaches (IPCC, SRES, CPB), adapted and detailed to service our goals.

 

Basics of sustainable development- People, Planet, Profit
sustainable development where economics, ecology and socio-cultural aspects should develop in balance and mutual interest is guiding in selecting topics and valuating outcomes.

 

From Drivers to changing conditions, effects and response.
Drivers are "external or outside forces" exerting influence on Europe's rural area and causing changes in People, Planet, Profit indicators. Major drivers are shown in the figure. Driving processes are interrelated: climate change depends on economic growth; economic growth relates to demograhy or technology, etcetera.

 

- World economy/welfare: demand and supply, exchange of goods and services. These determine the flow of labour, income, consumption patterns, capital investment and many other factors.

 

- Climate change and related conditions: shifts in precipitation, temperature and water discharge (flooding) or sea level rise bring various risks or opportunities.

 

- Technology: has significant effects on land use and other aspects (mobility, communication). As such it is hard to include in models; dissemination of new technology can be assumed more easily.

 

- (Geo-)political change implies change in international politics and policy making. The increasing role of international governance, international treaties (such as WTO, Agreements on Biodiversity or Environment, Kyoto). On continental level the formation and expansion of the EU.

 

- Consumer patterns are sometimes hard to define, but are influential: e.g. consumption patterns (diet changes), an increased awareness of ecological or social problems or concerns on animal welfare.

 


Core models
Models help to assess effects assuming changes in driving forces. Models are fed with data, follow formal rules to simulate interactions and give output in desired format (e.g. tables, graphs, maps). Three "state of the art" core models are at the heart and were linked for specific EURURALIS goals. Other models are used to specify effects on certain indicators.

 

-GTAP / LEITAP
The Global Trade Analysis Project (GTAP) model was adapted for EURURALIS(LEITAP). It simulates world trade for all economic sectors (agriculture including processing industry, services).   read more about GTAP/LEITAP >>

-IMAGE
The IMAGE model (Integrated Model to Assess the Global Environment) assesses effects of global change on the environment. Spatial resolution is mostly on national level.  read more about IMAGE >>


-CLUE
The CLUE model (the Conversion of Land Use and its Effects; CLUE-s version 2.3) is used to allocate the predicted changes in land use at a 1 km2 resolution.  read more about CLUE>>

 

Indicators 3 P
Indicators are selected variables for the various domains of People, Planet and Profit. In addition to single value indicators, we presented some meta-indicators. These are aggregated or generalized, to a higher level, based upon underlying EURURALIS data.
