EURURALIS has elaborated four scenarios, based on two, more or less independent “dimensions”:
(a) the type or degree of international cooperation (regional vs global) and
(b) the degree of government intervention (extensive vs limited).

 

The four scenarios have a time horizon of 30 years (incidentally more) and operate in time steps of 10 years. They start on a global scale before zooming in to the European Union; hence taking into consideration the effects on Europe of developments world wide and vice versa (e.g. regarding population, economic growth and trade policies).

 

The evolution of many driving forces is highly uncertain. Yet, policy makers need to anticipate on new challenges and opportunities. They need information on relationships as well: where to intervene effectively without causing adverse side-effects? The best scientists can offer is images of conceivable rather than probable futures.

 

Such explorative scenarios help to understand the significance of contrasting but equally plausible developments that could happen to the institutions involved; how these developments are related to trends in driving forces and to alternative cultural and political trends, conceivably starting off from the facts and fashions of today.
