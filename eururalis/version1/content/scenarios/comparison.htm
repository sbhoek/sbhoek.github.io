From world visions to the EU level: intentions compared

We compared the various scenarios, i.e. world visions,а to the various policy goals set by the EU and gave scores for the weight a certain policy goal receives in a certain scenario. As an example: a world vision oriented on a free market puts competitiveness of EU's agriculture on the forefront, a more protective scenario like Regional Communities (B2) emphasizes environmental goals. We stress that this overview is a picture of intentions within the context of a world vision. In practice it will turn out that some intentions or goals are hard to realize in a certain strategy within a world vision. Scenario Global Economy (A1) could yield a higher score on biodiversity than scenario Regional Communities (B2), though the intentions could suggest otherwise.

In each scenario policies evolve in different directions, depending on prevailing world views and the degree in which these can be realised. We give some more detail for agricultural policy and policy for rural areas:

In the Continental Markets scenario (A2) government intervention regarding agriculture remains rather high due to prevailing national interests impeding reforms at EU level and the feeling that border measures in accordance with WTO are insufficient to protect EU agriculture.
