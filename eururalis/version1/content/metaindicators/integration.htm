Agricultural Production Share

Share of agri-food complex in economy keeps on falling in all scenarios. The higher the welfare level the more important industry and services, the lower the share of the agri-food complex. In EU10 decrease in share of agri-food complex is therefore higher than in EU15

Agricultural income in relation to average income

Income growth in agri-food complex is lower than average income, especially in Global (A1 and B1) scenarios.

Decrease of CAP expenses

CAP expenditures reduced in global scenarios (A1 and B1). Expenditures are stopped or increase in regional scenarios (A2 and B2). EU10 receives more CAP payments.

Food self-sufficiency

Changes in self-sufficiency of food complex are limited in all scenarios. A higher income growth in the EU 10 leads to a slightly lower self-suffiency as demand for food is higher. Self sufficiency potential in the EU15 is higher in the A1 scenario as food demand growth in developing countries stimulates production in the EU15.

Population of rural areas

Population of rural areas is decreasing strongly in all scenario, almost at the same rate. According to our data there is no substantial difference between the EU15 and the EU10. Ageing is a related feature.

Cultural-Historical Landscape quality

Most of the cultural-historical landscape will be influenced by the transformation of arable land. Scenario A1 shows the largest average area change into urban landuse.

Biodiversity 'Nature'

The area of nature increases slightly in all scenarios but A2: in 2030 area B1> B2 > A1 > 2000. The average biodiversity value of nature drops in all scenarios due to biotope loss, climate change, pollution. Largest decrease in EU10 is in A1. Lowest decrease in EU15 in B1. The scenarios rank A1(lowest biodiversity) < A2 < B2 < B1


Support Kyoto protocol

As most scenarios foresee a certain amount of land abandonment, expansion of forests could be expected. Building up biomass (carbon sequestration) supports Kyoto targets in a very modest way.


Landdegradation

Soil degradation risk decreases for all scenarios. Diffrences are reflected in the assigned scores. It improves from A2, A1 to the B scenarios. Southern Europe will improve significantly. Central Europe shows only a minor improvement.

Biodiversity 'Agriculture'

The scores were set at: 0 = -15% change A biodiversity increment of +f 5% is an extremely better situation based beacuse agricultural production systems should be extensified by 50% or conventional farming (50%) is converted into organic farming. An increment of –5% will lead to an extremely worse situation. (10-15% ecosystem quality decrease is more or less the change between the pre-industrial 1850 and current 2000 production systems)

Biodiversity 'Grassland'

Grasslands (extensive) are declining in area and quality due to land abandonment, climate change and increased environmental stress. A1 shows the strongest decline. B2 the least.


Critical load exceedance

The exeedance of critical Nitrogen loads relate to the areas affected and the amount of exeedance. Especially in EU10 countries intensifying agriculture and emissions from households, industry and traffic cause increasing stress on ecosystems.
