The desired cohesion within the enlarged EU (15 +10) poses questions on how EU10 and EU 15 countries will experience future developments: Is there a converging or diverging course ?


EU 10 countries take advantage of the accession in a general economical sense. Rural economies however will be affected strongly and at high rates by a decreasing share of agriculture in economy, compared to the industrial and services sectors. There will be less room for small farmers.

 

Restructuring of agriculture, the expected expulsion of labour, migration to cities and modernization of agriculture will affect rural areas and communities strongly, the more outspoken in the A1 scenario, the least in the B2 scenario.

 

These changes will affect rural economy (farmers income lagging behind compared to average income), social and cultural aspects that together cause lower viability in rural communities) and ecological aspects (less biodiversity, higher environmental stress) in a direction comparable to what many EU15 nations experienced earlier, but at a much higher rate.

 

The overall message seems to be : catching up but at various costs !
