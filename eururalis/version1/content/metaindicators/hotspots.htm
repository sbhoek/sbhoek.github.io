Large differences in land use dynamics exist between scenarios
The demands for agricultural and urban land and the varying ambitions of spatial policies lead to contrasting dynamics in land use between scenarios. Table 1 indicates which area percentage of the EU will see a change between 2000 and 2030. Even in the scenario with the smallest dynamics (A2) almost 5% of the total land area will be converted. This only includes conversions within the legend classes. Conversions between crop types, extensive and intensive use or between residential or industrial and infrastructural functions are not counted. Considerable land abandonment makes the B1 scenario the most dynamic, affecting the aesthetic and functional quality of the cultural landscapes. In the A2 and B2 scenarios the dynamics are much lower. Whereas the accession countries show more dynamics in the A scenarios compared to the EU15 countries, the pattern is opposite in the B scenarios where most dynamics occur in the EU15 countries.

Land abandonment is most important land use change
Land abandonment of agricultural land is the most important area change. In the A2 scenario 2.5% of the land area (which equals approx. 5% of the agricultural area in 2000) is abandoned, against 6.4% (app. 13% of the agricultural area) in the A1 scenario. Land abandonment poses important dilemmas for policy makers. In the A1 scenario much land is used for residential, industrial and recreation purposes. In all other scenarios urban development is less giving opportunities for nature. In the A scenarios nature development is assumed to occur spontaneous. In the B scenario active nature development occurs leads to large expansion of the natural areas, though patterns differ (B1 more concentrated as a consequence of the Natura policy).

Maps show that spatial patterns of land use change are sensitive to spatial policies
Three maps show the overlap per main land use changes for all scenarios. Where overlap is observed land use change occurs independent from the scenario conditions and thus from different spatial policies. Many other areas, however change in just one or two scenarios, partly or largely due to differences in spatial policy. The maximum overlap for urbanization is restricted due to low urbanization in B2 and the fact that urbanization patterns differ strongly between A and B scenarios. Land abandonment and new nature differ even more. Only 39% of the new nature area in the A2 scenario is also converted to nature in the other scenarios. This is mainly due to the largely different spatial policies concerning nature protection and enforcement of attention for the Natura2000 structure versus the maintenance of natural patches within the agricultural landscapes.
