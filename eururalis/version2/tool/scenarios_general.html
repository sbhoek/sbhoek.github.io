Because the future is uncertain, EUruralis 2.0 is built on four scenarios. These scenarios represent uncertainties in as to how the world might develop and in policy choices.

Key driving forces within scenarios are political developments on both European and global scales, macro-economic growth and demographic developments. Other important drivers are technology (The Internet was not foreseen 30 years ago), climate change (to be influenced in the long run, but not for the coming 15-20 years) and consumer behaviour. EU and national policies are also important.

The four scenarios also relate to different plausible developments defined by two axes. The vertical axis symbolizes a global approach as opposed to a more regional approach to problems and strategies. The horizontal axis represents an open market compared with a higher level of intervention and regulation by governments.
