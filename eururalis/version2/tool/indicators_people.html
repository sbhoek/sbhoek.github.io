Employment (Index)
The number of employees in a region standardized as full time employees, as percentage of the number of employees in 2001. This indicator develops to a large extent parallel with population growth. Percentage change in employment is calculated on a national level and downscaled is accomplished by applying those percentages, corrected for the region being lagged or advanced, to the regional employment levels.

Value added per farmer - Real value added per worker in agriculture (Index)
Value added per worker in agriculture as a percentage of the value added per worker in 2001. It is deflated by the national GDP deflator. It indicates the growth in welfare of farmers. Be aware that the indicator includes not only income from labor, but also from capital and land. This indicator is calculated on a national level, and it is the same for all regions within a country.

Self-sufficiency (Index)
Total primary agricultural production in a region as a percentage of total regional consumption. If this is smaller than 100, then the region has to import agricultural products from other regions. If it is larger, it is a net exporter. This indicator is calculated on a national level, and it is the same for all regions within a country.
