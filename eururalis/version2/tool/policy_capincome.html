Common Agricultural Policy - Income Support

Agricultural and trade policies are important factors that shape the future of agriculture and rural areas. These policies are characterized by a complex interplay between export subsidies, import tariffs, production quotas, tariff rates and may include decoupled or coupled income transfers. The current Common Agricultural Policy (CAP) will be in place until 2013. After 2013 the CAP might change fundamentally causing major effects on agriculture, land-use and livestock patterns.

In Eururalis the policy option for the CAP is split into two separate options. The first option concerns the CAP Market Support and is described in the previous sheet.

The second policy option for CAP is the CAP Income Support. There are four settings for this option:

  1. Abolishment of all income support, which will take place after 2010
  2. Decreasing income support, which means that the budget for income support will be reduced by 50% until 2030
  3. Stable income support which means no change in the budget for income support until 2030
  4. Increasing income support which means that the budget for income support will be increased by 50% until 2030
